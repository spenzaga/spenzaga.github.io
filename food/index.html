Welcome to "Containers & Quantities"!

In this lesson, you will learn how to talk about food and drinks using the right containers and quantity words.

📦 Containers:

a bottle, a glass, a cup, a bowl, a bag, a box, a can, a carton, a jar, a packet

🔢 Quantities:

some, any, a lot of, a little, a few, a piece of, a slice of, a loaf of

These words help us describe exactly how much food or drink we want!

🔢 Quantity Words

some I have some water. (positive sentences)
any Do you have any milk? (questions/negatives)
a lot of There are a lot of apples.
a little I need a little sugar. (uncountable)
a few There are a few eggs. (countable)

❓ Questions & Answers

How to Ask About Containers & Quantities:

Q: How much water do you need?

A: I need a bottle of water.

Q: How many eggs are there?

A: There are a few eggs in the box.

Q: Is there any milk in the fridge?

A: Yes, there is a carton of milk.

Q: What would you like to drink?

A: I'd like a cup of tea, please.

Q: Do you have any sugar?

A: Yes, I have a little sugar.

💡 Remember!

How much → for uncountable nouns (water, sugar, rice)

How many → for countable nouns (eggs, apples, bottles)

📝 Text Example

🛒 My Shopping List

Today is Saturday. My mother and I go to the supermarket. We need to buy some things for the week.

Here is our shopping list:

  • Two bottles of water
  • A carton of milk
  • A loaf of bread
  • A bag of rice (5 kg)
  • A jar of strawberry jam
  • A box of cereal
  • A dozen eggs
  • A few bananas
  • A can of tuna
  • Some vegetables

We don't need any sugar because we still have a lot of sugar at home. We also have a little butter left in the fridge.

At the store, I ask my mother: "Do we need any juice?" She says: "Yes, please get a carton of orange juice."

💬 Dialog: True or False?

How to Make a Cup of Tea ☕

Anna: Hey Ben, would you like a cup of tea?

Ben: Yes, please! How do you make it?

Anna: First, boil a kettle of water.

Ben: Okay, then what?

Anna: Put a tea bag in a cup. Pour the hot water into the cup.

Ben: Do I add sugar?

Anna: Add a spoonful of sugar if you like. Then add a little milk.

Ben: That sounds easy! Thanks, Anna!

🎭 Dialog Scenarios

🗣️ Asking & Giving Information

Useful Expressions:

Asking:

"Can I have a bottle of water, please?"

Giving:

"Here you are. One bottle of water."

Asking:

"How much sugar do you need?"

Giving:

"Just a little, please."

Asking:

"Is there any juice left?"

Giving:

"Yes, there's a carton of juice in the fridge."

Asking:

"Would you like some cake?"

Giving:

"Yes, please. Just a slice."

💡 Tip: Be polite!

Always use "please" and "thank you" when asking for food and drink.

Warming Up

☀️ Let's Think!

Have you ever helped your mother or father cook in the kitchen? What did you cook? 🤔

When we cook, we follow steps. For example, to make a cup of tea, we need to boil water, put a tea bag in a cup, pour hot water, and add sugar. These steps are called a procedure!

🍽️ Discussion Questions

1. What is your favourite food? Can you name the ingredients?
2. Do you know how to make fried rice? What do you need?
3. Have you ever read a recipe in English before?
4. Why is it important to follow the steps in order?

💡 Real-life connection: Recipes are procedure texts you use every day! When you search "how to make instant noodles" on YouTube, that is a procedure text in video form.

How to Make Fried Rice

Goal: How to make delicious fried rice
Materials:
  • A plate of cooked rice
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 cloves of garlic (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • A pinch of salt and pepper
  • Spring onions (sliced)
Steps:
  1. First, heat the cooking oil in a frying pan.
  2. Then, crack the eggs and scramble them in the pan.
  3. Next, add the chopped garlic and stir until fragrant.
  4. After that, add the cooked rice and mix it well.
  5. Then, pour the soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir evenly.
  6. Finally, add the spring onions and serve on a plate.
🍜

How to Make Instant Noodles

Goal: How to make instant noodles
Materials:
  • A packet of instant noodles
  • 400 ml of water
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • Vegetables: cabbage, carrot (sliced)
Steps:
  1. First, boil 400 ml of water in a pot.
  2. Then, put the noodles into the boiling water.
  3. Next, cook for about 3 minutes and stir occasionally.
  4. After that, add the seasoning and mix well.
  5. Then, add the egg and vegetables if you like.
  6. Finally, pour the noodles into a bowl and serve.

Text Structure

📐 Generic Structure of Procedure Text

1. Goal / Title
Tells the reader what they will make or do.
2. Materials / Ingredients
Lists the things you need.
3. Steps / Methods
Explains the steps in order.

🔍 Identify the Structure

Read the text below. The structure is highlighted:

🏷️ Goal: How to Make a Cup of Tea
📦 Materials: A tea bag, hot water, sugar, a cup, a spoon
📝 Steps:
1. First, boil the water.
2. Then, put the tea bag in a cup.
3. Next, pour the hot water into the cup.
4. After that, add sugar and stir.
5. Finally, remove the tea bag and enjoy!
Grammar Focus

📖 Imperative Sentences

Imperative sentences give commands or instructions. They start with a verb (action word). We use them a lot in recipes!

✅ Cut the onion into small pieces.
✅ Boil the water for 5 minutes.
✅ Add two tablespoons of sugar.
✅ Stir the mixture well.
✅ Don't forget to wash your hands!

🔗 Sequence Connectives

We use these words to show the order of steps:

First Second Then Next After that Finally
Example:
First, wash the vegetables.
Then, cut them into small pieces.
Next, heat the oil in a pan.
After that, fry the vegetables for 5 minutes.
Finally, serve on a plate.
Dialogue: True or False

Asking & Giving Information

🗣️ Useful Expressions

Asking:

"How do you make ___?"
"Can you tell me how to make ___?"
"What do I need to make ___?"
"What is the first/next step?"

Giving Information:

"You need ___ and ___."
"First, you have to ___."
"Then, ___ and after that ___."
"Finally, ___."

🥞 Example: How to Make a Pancake

Sari: Can you tell me how to make a pancake?
Doni: Sure! You need flour, eggs, milk, sugar, and butter.
Sari: What is the first step?
Doni: First, mix the flour, sugar, and eggs in a bowl. Then, add milk and stir well.
Sari: What do I do next?
Doni: Next, heat butter in a pan. After that, pour the batter and cook each side for 2 minutes. Finally, serve with honey!

How to Make a Banana Smoothie

Ingredients: 2 ripe bananas, 200 ml of milk, 2 tablespoons of honey, 5 ice cubes, a blender.

Steps:
First, peel the bananas and cut them into pieces. Then, put the bananas, milk, and honey into the blender. Next, add the ice cubes. After that, blend everything until smooth. Finally, pour the smoothie into a glass and enjoy!
